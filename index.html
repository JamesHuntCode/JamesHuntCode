About Me

Computer Science Student & Software Engineer

I am a software engineer based in Plymouth, England and I currently study BSc (Hons) Computer Science at the University of Plymouth. I have been programming incessantly for the better part of the last six years and I have no intent of stopping any time soon. My passion for software engineering and Computer Science has allowed me to do some amazing things during the time I have been studying and I can honestly say that there is no field in which I would rather work.

I am the current Chairman and a founding member of Plymouth University's Computer Society (CompSoc). Our main goals are to create a student network for anyone with an interest in the computing field and to create connections between students and employers. We do this by hosting regular talks from individuals working in industry followed by question and answer sessions. We hosted our first Hackaton in April of 2018 and look forward to hosting many more.

Alongside studying and helping to run CompSoc, I am a PALS (Peer Assisted Learning) Leader for Plymouth University's BSc (Hons) Computing course and I work part-time at Vodafone UK.

Recent Work & Interests

My Recent Work

I have recently been invited to work on a mobile application commencing July 2018 in partnership with Plymouth University's Medical Faculty. The aim of this specific application is to help gather data regaring individuals with Multiple Sclerosis. The app is a "daily mobile diary" designed to help medical experts collect valuable data surrounding how many falls a person has experienced that day and if it has resulted in an injury.
