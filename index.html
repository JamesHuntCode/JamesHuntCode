About Me

Computer Science Student & Software Engineer

I am a software engineer based in Plymouth, England. I am about to enroll into second year BSc (Hons) Computer Science at the University of Plymouth. I have been programming incessantly for the better part of the last six years and I have no intent of stopping any time soon. My passion for software engineering and Computer Science has allowed me to do some amazing things during the time I have been studying. I can honestly say that there is no field in which I would rather work.

I am the current Chairman and a founding member of Plymouth University's Computer Society (CompSoc). Our main goals are to create a student network for anyone with an interest in the computing field and create connections between students and employers. We do this by hosting regular talks from individuals working in industry followed by question and answer sessions. We also hosted our first Hackaton April 2018 and look forward to hosting many more.

Alongside all of the above I work part-time at Vodafone UK.

My Recent Work & Interests

I am currently developing an Android mobile application for captains of sports teams. This app is designed to make the process of laying out a new team and organizing the players a far simpler, easier process. I have also been invited to work on another mobile app commencing July 2018 in partnership with Plymouth Plymouth University's Medical Faculty. The aim of this specific application is to help individuals with Multiple Sclerosis. When the person falls and requires instant help, this app will be the solution allowing the individual to contact someone on the push of a button.

As of recent, I am interested in mobile application development. I have been using Android Studio to develop native Android applications and Xamarin to develop cross-OS applications. I would really like to branch out and learn native iOS development also. There are a whole list of topics in Computer Science that I am currently intersted in, luckily for me, my second year at university covers almost all of them. Some of these areas include; Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning, Computation Theory, Object Oriented Software Engineering with Java, Web Application Development.
